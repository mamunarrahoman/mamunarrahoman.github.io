About Me

Hi, I am Md. Mamunar Rahoman Roton — a VLSI engineer with hands-on experience in IC Physical Design and backend implementation flows, including floorplanning, CTS, place & route, timing closure, and physical verification using industry EDA tools at advanced technology nodes.

I am particularly interested in digital system design, RTL development (SystemVerilog), and computer architecture, especially CPU/GPU microarchitecture and high-performance, low-power computing systems. My goal is to bridge architecture and physical implementation, contributing to efficient and scalable semiconductor designs as a future Digital System Design Architect.

Research

Undergraduate Thesis: Applications to Rectilinear Steiner Minimum Trees for Higher Degree Nets with Advanced Net-breaking Techniques.

  • Compressed and optimized the existing RSMT (Rectilinear Steiner Minimum Tree) database using orientation-based methodologies to improve efficiency and reduce redundancy.
  • Applied advanced net-breaking techniques to enable scalable and accurate routing for higher-degree nets in Physical Design Automation.
  • Developed a methodology to generate multiple routing solution paths from placement data instead of relying on a single reference solution, expanding design flexibility.
  • Enhanced routing efficiency and runtime performance by introducing alternative routing strategies for critical paths using the newly developed multi-solution database.
